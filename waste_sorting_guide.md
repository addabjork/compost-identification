# 🗑️ Waste Sorting Guide (New York City)

---

## 1. General Trash — Refuse / Landfill

Garbage that **cannot** be recycled or composted. Usually goes in a **black garbage bag** or lidded trash can.

**Common examples:**
- Dirty paper towels
- Diapers
- Pet feces
- Foam
- Oil-stained paper
- Ceramics
- Mirrors
- Light bulbs

---

## 2. Recyclables — Recycle ♻️

Recyclables should be as **clean, dry, and emptied** as possible before putting them out.

### 📄 Paper & Cardboard
- Newspapers
- Express / shipping boxes
- Cartons
- Office paper
- Paper bags

### 🥫 Metal
- Aluminum cans
- Food cans
- Tin foil
- Small metal containers

### 🍶 Glass
- Glass bottles
- Glass jars

### 🧴 Plastic
- Plastic bottles and containers (usually marked with a recycling symbol on the bottom)
- Note: Some areas have additional restrictions on **plastic bags** and **foam plastics**

---

## 3. Compost — Organics 🌱

New York City now **mandates** organic waste classification (Organics / Compost).

### 🍎 Food Scraps
- Fruits and vegetables
- Meats and bones
- Dairy products
- Cooked foods

### 🧻 Food-Soiled Paper
- Napkins and paper towels
- Tea bags
- Paper plates
- Coffee filters
- Pizza boxes

### 🍂 Yard Waste
- Leaves
- Grass clippings
- Twigs and branches
- Plants

---

## 4. Special Waste ⚠️

These items **cannot** be thrown directly into ordinary trash cans and must be disposed of separately.

### 🔋 Batteries
- Rechargeable batteries
- Button batteries
- Mobile phone batteries

### 📱 Electronic Waste (E-Waste)
- Mobile phones
- Computers
- Televisions
- Printers

### ☣️ Hazardous Waste
- Paint
- Detergents
- Engine oil
- Pesticides
- Chemicals

### 🛋️ Bulk Items
- Furniture
- Mattresses
- Carpets
- Home appliances

> **Note:** Bulk items usually require a **reservation** or must be placed according to specific local regulations.
